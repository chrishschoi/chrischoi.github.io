Tourism & Hospitality Research · Practice

Evidence-based tourism & hospitality insights for curious professionals.

I am HS Chris Choi, Professor in the School of Hospitality, Food and Tourism Management at the University of Guelph. I study consumer behavior, sustainable tourism, ESG, and AI-driven service experiences, working with industry, students, and policy makers to translate research into practice.

I have published more than 200 peer-reviewed articles and technical reports and typically co-author 8 to 10 journal papers per year in tourism, hospitality, and service management.

Focus areas

Themes that repeatedly appear in my research and consulting work.

Consumer behavior in tourism & food

Decision making around travel, dining, and risk: pricing, fairness, tipping, loyalty, and resistance to new service models.

Service management & experience design

Designing high-impact customer journeys in hotels, restaurants, OTAs, and attractions, including service failure and recovery.

Sustainability, ESG & responsible tourism

How guests, residents, and employees respond to ESG initiatives, regenerative tourism, and climate-related business resilience.
